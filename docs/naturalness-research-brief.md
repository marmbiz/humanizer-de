# Naturalness Research Brief

Status: P0 research safeguard. This file defines how research claims are allowed to enter the audit. It is not yet a full literature review.

## Scope

The Humanizer (Deutsch) audit optimizes German text quality: naturalness, precision, reader guidance, register fidelity, meaning preservation, and substance. It does not optimize for AI-detector evasion, deceptive authorship claims, or synthetic "more human" noise.

## Claim Classes

| Claim class | Use in docs | Required evidence |
|---|---|---|
| External evidence | Findings about research, best practices, detector limits, plain language, stylometry, readability, or German text simplification | Named source, link or bibliographic anchor, checked date, and a short note on applicability to German text |
| Repo heuristic | Humanizer-de rule, pattern, decision table, or scenario expectation | Repo file path and rationale; do not present as externally validated research |
| Operational guardrail | Instruction that protects evidence, register, meaning, or proportional edits | Repo file path, test/scenario if available, and failure mode |
| Open question | Plausible improvement without enough evidence | Mark as hypothesis; do not promote to roadmap blocker without validation |

## Research Rules

- Do not write "Studien zeigen", "Forschung belegt", or similar authority phrases unless the source is named and checked.
- Prefer primary or high-quality sources for research claims: peer-reviewed papers, official standards, language/plain-language bodies, or clearly scoped empirical work.
- Separate German-specific evidence from English-language transfer. A finding from English stylometry is not automatically a German writing rule.
- Separate detector limitations from text-quality guidance. Detector behavior may explain a risk, but it is not an optimization target.
- Do not cite AI-humanizer marketing pages as evidence for quality claims.
- Do not infer semantic preservation from surface metrics. Current repo tooling can detect anchor drift and contract violations, not full semantic equivalence.
- When evidence is mixed or weak, say so directly.

## Unsourced Idea Intake

Ideas submitted as planning notes, examples, or "scientific mechanisms" are not external evidence until they are sourced. They may still be useful as internal heuristics after filtering through the project safeguards.

Accepted as internal heuristics only:

- Report deterministic preflight bands for KI-artige Oberflächencluster when framed as text-quality risk, not authorship proof.
- Stabilize speaker position and address form when the input or target profile supports it.
- Prefer verbs over nominalized process nouns when the actor and action are present.
- Replace mechanical logical connectors with content-led transitions where the relation is already clear.
- Use modal particles only in Locker mode and only when the target voice carries them.

Rejected as unsafe defaults:

- "Maximize entropy" as a goal.
- Add sentence fragments, grammar breaks, slang, or rare words to lower predictability.
- Add `ich`, `wir`, `du`, anecdotes, emotion, or opinion without input/context anchors.
- Treat passive voice, `man`, formal register, or Nominalstil as AI evidence in Formal, legal, technical, academic, or institutional text.

## Roadmap Ideas

- Calibrated AI-involvement audit: possible future mode for asking whether AI was likely involved. Keep it out of the active skill until there is a labeled German benchmark, a documented calibration method, and known false-positive/false-negative rates. If built later, it should report evidence-density bands with uncertainty, not authorship proof or uncalibrated percentages.

## Required Framing For Future Audit Docs

Every artifact created from this research brief should include the same boundary:

> Humanizer-de improves German text quality and reduces generic LLM prose patterns. It must not be used to fabricate experience, hide authorship, evade detectors, or weaken factual precision for a more "human" surface.
